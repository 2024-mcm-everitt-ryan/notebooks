The GSOC Operator is responsible for providing primary and secondary security service support by following pre-defined actions to handle both normal course of business activities and critical events, including escalation to key parties within the operations centre and other support groups within the company. The GSOC Operator uses a variety of tools that can range from enterprise access control and alarm monitoring systems to various business intelligence sources, open source media, internet, and in-house tools to respond and alert personnel regarding any adverse conditions that may affect the client, its employees, facilities, and/or properties.

Qualifications:
- Associate's degree in Criminal Justice, Communications or related field and/or 2 years of relevant work experience.
- Must be able to pass any Provincial-required training or other qualifications for employment.
- Must possess a valid driver's license.

Asset Qualifications:
- Professional training and/or education in one or more of the following:
  - Customer service
  - Phone and communications etiquette
  - Dispatch operations
- No specific licenses/certifications required, but professional security certifications such as the Certified Protection Officer (CPO), Certified Protection Professional (CPP), or a communications certification strongly encouraged.
- Experience in a modern 911 Call Centre, Central Station Alarm Monitoring and Dispatch Operation, Technical Security Control Room, or similar environment is a plus.
- Prior military or law enforcement experience is a plus.
