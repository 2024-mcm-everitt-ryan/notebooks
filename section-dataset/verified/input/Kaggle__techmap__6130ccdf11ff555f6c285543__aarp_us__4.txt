Overview:  
Maintain mechanical equipment and facilities and aid in the manufacturing of all company products in a safe and sanitary work environment.

Qualifications:  
- High School Diploma or GED.  
- One-year certificate from college or technical school, or two to three years related experience and/or training, or equivalent combination of education and experience.  
- Must be able to possess and maintain a valid forklift license issued by the company.  
- Experience within the food and beverage industry preferred.  
- Able to add, subtract, multiply, and divide in all units of measure, using whole numbers, common fractions, and decimals.  
- Overtime and working on holidays is often required.

Physical Demands:  
While performing the duties of this job, the employee is regularly required to:  
- Stand, use hands, and stretch and reach with hands and arms.  
- Frequently walk, stoop, kneel, crouch, or crawl.  
- Climb and talk or hear.  
- Occasionally climb or balance and smell.  
- Frequently lift and/or move up to 10 pounds and occasionally lift and/or move more than 100 pounds.  
- Specific vision abilities required by this job include close vision, distance vision, color vision, peripheral vision, depth perception, and ability to adjust focus.  
- Must have full range of body motion.  
- Regularly exposed to moving mechanical parts.  
- Frequently exposed to wet and/or humid conditions and risk of electrical shock.  
- Occasionally exposed to high, precarious places, fumes or airborne particles, toxic or caustic chemicals, outside weather conditions, and extreme cold.  
- The noise level in the work environment is usually loud.

Responsibilities:  
- Ability to disassemble and assemble all parts of machines and support equipment according to the manufacturers' specifications.  
- Troubleshoot and repair all equipment in the plant.  
- Perform preventative maintenance on all equipment as scheduled.  
- Have a basic understanding of hydraulic systems.  
- Arc welding and/or Heliarc welding.  
- Electrical troubleshooting and repair.  
- Electronic and PLC troubleshooting and repair.  
- Rebuild gearboxes and drives.  
- Repair and troubleshoot filling machines.  
- Rebuild valves and pumps.  
- Ensure full compliance with all company safety policies and procedures.  
- Access all areas of machines and support equipment.  
- Access all areas of the facilities as needed to perform repairs and maintenance.  
- Provide own hand tools.
