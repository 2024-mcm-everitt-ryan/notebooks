Permanent Full & Part Time Clinical Support Specialists

We are looking for two enthusiastic and proactive Health professionals (OTs, Physiotherapists, Social Workers, and RNs) with knowledge and/or experience in TBIs (traumatic brain injuries) and complex PTSD or similar relevant experience, preferably within the community. The higher than normal remuneration for this role reflects the complexities of the role and the clinical skills required to support the client to live a fun and meaningful life with her brain injury-related challenges. Rates range from $44 - $66 per hour.

Understanding of TBI and Complex PTSD and how this can affect a person's ability to function is essential to be able to help the client to compensate for the challenges following her injury. You will assist the client with all her activities of daily living in her own home. The client is mobile and active in her community. She regularly participates in voluntary work and social activities.

This is an exciting role for a Health professional who is looking to expand or diversify their profile by being part of a team providing 24-hour support and care to the client. The role is more practice-based and practical, supporting a female who requires assistance with her daily tasks and activities following a brain injury, with minimal administration requirements and no report writing!

The successful candidate will have a kind and caring nature, remain calm under pressure, be comfortable around dogs, and enjoy working independently as well as part of a dynamic team. Become part of the fantastic support team that helps her to live as meaningful a life as possible following her injury. You must be willing to work up to 3 shifts a week and be flexible with additional shifts if required. Shifts are 12 hours with a 1-hour handover, 7 days per week, with both weekend/weekday and day/night awake shifts available. The successful candidate must be willing to work weekends and public holidays if required.

The successful candidate:
- Applicant must be a qualified Health Professional e.g. Social Worker, Occupational Therapist, Physiotherapist, Nurse, and associated allied health professionals.
- A minimum of 3 years of experience in working with traumatic brain injury and trauma as a Health Professional is required.
- Community experience will be an advantage.
- A friendly, professional manner with good written and oral communication skills and a kind, caring, and gentle approach is essential.
