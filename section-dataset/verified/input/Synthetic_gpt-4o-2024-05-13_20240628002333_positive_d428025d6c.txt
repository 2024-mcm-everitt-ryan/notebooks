Company Background:
Kelly, Williams and Thomas is a premier global law firm specializing in human rights advocacy. We champion the rights of individuals and communities, ensuring justice and equality for all.

Job Title:
Human Rights Specialist

Job Description:
We are seeking a passionate Human Rights Specialist to join our dynamic team. This role will focus on promoting and defending the rights of marginalized communities, including the LGBTQ+ community. Our firm values diverse perspectives and experiences that enhance our advocacy efforts.

Responsibilities:
- Conduct thorough research on human rights issues and develop strategic advocacy plans.
- Engage with stakeholders, including NGOs, government bodies, and community organizations.
- Draft, review, and present reports, proposals, and policy recommendations.
- Provide training and support to community advocates.
- Represent the firm in public forums, conferences, and media engagements.

Qualifications and Experience:
- Bachelor's degree in Law, Human Rights, Political Science, or related field; advanced degree preferred.
- Minimum of 5 years experience in human rights advocacy or related field.
- Strong understanding and commitment to LGBTQ+ rights.
- Excellent research, writing, and communication skills.
- Ability to work collaboratively in a diverse team environment.

Perks and Benefits:
- We are committed to fair and equitable pay practices. The salary for this position ranges from $70,000 to $95,000, based on your experience and skills.
- Comprehensive healthcare package, including dental and vision.
- Generous paid time off and flexible work hours.
- Opportunities for professional development and career advancement.
- Supportive and inclusive work environment promoting diversity and equality.

Application Process:
We strive to build a workforce that reflects the diversity of the LGBTQ+ community. Only candidates identifying as gay, lesbian, bisexual, transgender, or queer should apply. Please submit your resume, cover letter, and three professional references.
